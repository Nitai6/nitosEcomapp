# Content Scraping & Sourcing — Theme-Page Operator Manual

How to legally, efficiently, and at scale source content for faceless theme pages across all 7 platforms.

## The 5 sourcing tiers (ordered by cost + risk)

| Tier | Source | Cost | Risk | Best for |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Public domain + Creative Commons** | Free | Zero | Stock B-roll, historical footage, classical music |
| 2 | **Stock libraries (licensed)** | $15-50/mo | Zero | Original-looking pages with curated visuals |
| 3 | **AI-generated content** | $20-100/mo | Low (disclosure required some platforms) | Faceless YT, voiceover Shorts, Pinterest at scale |
| 4 | **Re-edit with permission/credit** | Free–$50/clip | Low–Medium | Niche-tactical content where the creator's idea is the value |
| 5 | **Re-upload (the gray zone)** | Free | High (strikes, suspensions, copyright claims) | Last resort — see legal section |

**Default policy**: 60% Tier 2-3 (stock + AI), 30% Tier 4 (permissioned re-edits), 10% original. Skip Tier 5 — short-term reach, long-term banned account.

## Tier 1 — Public domain + CC

### Footage
- **Pexels Videos** — free, CC0-equivalent, no attribution required
- **Pixabay Videos** — same
- **Videvo** — mix of free + paid, check license per clip
- **Coverr** — free curated B-roll
- **Mixkit** — free templates + footage
- **Internet Archive** (archive.org) — old films, TV, public domain footage (great for vintage/history theme pages)
- **NASA Image Library** — all space content, public domain
- **Library of Congress** — historical photos/film, mostly public domain

### Music
- **YouTube Audio Library** — free, no attribution required for most tracks
- **Free Music Archive** — CC-licensed
- **Pixabay Music** — free
- **Incompetech (Kevin MacLeod)** — CC-BY, attribution required

### Photos (for Pinterest, IG carousels, FB)
- **Unsplash, Pexels, Pixabay** — CC0
- **Wikimedia Commons** — CC + public domain (great for educational niches)

## Tier 2 — Licensed stock libraries

| Library | Cost | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| **Storyblocks** | $15-30/mo unlimited | All-purpose B-roll |
| **Artgrid** | $250/yr | Cinematic footage, premium look |
| **Envato Elements** | $16/mo | Templates + footage + music in one |
| **Epidemic Sound** | $15/mo | Music ONLY but cleared for monetization, the safest music license for YouTube/IG/TT |
| **Motion Array** | $30/mo | Templates + B-roll + music |
| **Pond5** | Pay-per-clip | Rare/specialty footage |

**Critical**: Epidemic Sound + Artlist are the two music libraries that **clear monetization on YouTube**. Don't use random royalty-free tracks — many are claimed by aggregators and your videos get demonetized.

## Tier 3 — AI generation

### Video
- **Pictory** — text/blog → video. Best for long-form YT.
- **Invideo AI** — text → video with stock + AI voice. Fast.
- **Runway ML / Sora (when accessible)** — original AI video clips
- **HeyGen / Synthesia** — AI avatar (NOT faceless — only if niche allows)
- **CapCut AI** — text-to-video built in, free

### Voice
- **ElevenLabs** — best AI voice, $5-22/mo. Use for faceless YT/Shorts narration.
- **Murf.ai** — alternative, slightly more robotic
- **PlayHT** — multilingual

### Image
- **Midjourney** — best aesthetic for IG/Pinterest niches (cottagecore, dark academia, sci-fi)
- **DALL-E 3 / GPT-4o image** — fastest, integrated with ChatGPT
- **Flux / Ideogram** — text-on-image (best for Pinterest pin headlines)
- **Leonardo.ai** — game/character art

### Disclosure
- **YouTube** requires disclosing AI-generated content if it depicts real people/events realistically (toggle in upload)
- **TikTok** has AI-generated content label (auto-applied for some tools)
- **Meta (IG/FB)** auto-labels AI content from major tools
- **Pinterest** no requirement yet

Always disclose if content depicts real people. Penalty for hidden AI = account-level demonetization.

## Tier 4 — Re-edit with permission/credit

### The DM-for-permission flow

The single highest-leverage growth tactic for theme pages. Most creators say yes if you ask properly.

**Template DM** (adjust per niche):
```
Hey [name], huge fan of your work.

I run @[your_page] — [niche] page, [follower count] followers.

Saw your [specific clip — be specific] and would love to share it with my audience.

Two ways I can credit you:
1. Tag in caption + first comment + on-screen handle for full duration
2. Whatever attribution format you prefer

Total revenue from these reposts goes back into more content for the niche. No monetization claim — happy to send any earnings tracking.

Sound okay?
```

**Tracking permissions**: Maintain `references/my-creator-permissions.md` per page — one row per creator with: handle, date, scope (any clip / specific clip / one-time), preferred attribution, contact, screenshot of consent.

### Re-edit techniques (avoiding "stale repost" detection)

Platforms downrank obvious cross-platform reposts (TikTok watermark on IG = capped reach). To re-upload safely:

1. **Strip metadata** — re-export through CapCut at different bitrate
2. **Crop differently** — 9:16 → reframed 9:16, 5-10% different framing
3. **Add overlays** — your text hooks, captions in your style
4. **Trim 0.3-0.5s from start + end** — different first/last frames
5. **Add intro frame** — your branded 0.3s opener
6. **Change audio** — replace music or add voiceover layer
7. **Color-grade differently** — slight LUT shift
8. **Speed change** — 1.0× → 1.05× or 0.95× (imperceptible to viewer, different to algorithm)

Apply 4+ of these and the platform sees it as a new asset.

## Tier 5 — Re-upload (gray zone)

Only use when:
- Niche convention allows it (compilation pages, news/recap pages)
- You credit on every post
- You're prepared for strikes

Risk profile:
- **YouTube**: 3 copyright strikes = channel deleted. Strict. Do not raw-repost.
- **TikTok**: warnings → bans, less aggressive than YT but still risky at scale
- **IG**: takedown of individual posts, rarely account-level unless serial offender
- **Pinterest**: low enforcement, but low value to repost since pin lifetime is long
- **Twitter/X**: low enforcement on raw text, image stealing gets DMCA'd
- **Bluesky**: community-driven enforcement, you'll get publicly called out
- **Facebook**: middling enforcement

Better strategy: get permission OR re-edit heavily (Tier 4).

### Manual scraping (zero cost)

For first 90 days you don't need paid tools:

1. **Find 10-20 top pages in your niche** — search hashtags, follow recommendations, Google "top [niche] pages instagram"
2. **Build a tracking sheet** (Google Sheet): page handle, follower count, niche sub-angle, post format, posting frequency, top 5 posts by engagement (manual — sort their grid by likes via 3rd-party tools or eyeball)
3. **Save best posts** — IG "save" feature, TT collections, YT playlists. Build a "swipe file" per niche.
4. **Weekly: extract patterns** — what hooks repeat? what formats dominate? what topics return?

### What to extract (the data model)

Per high-performing post, capture:
- Post URL
- Date posted
- Post format (Reel / carousel / Short / pin / thread / etc.)
- Length (for video) / word count (for text)
- Hook (first 3 seconds / first line)
- Body structure
- CTA used
- Hashtags
- Engagement (likes, comments, shares, saves, views)
- Engagement rate vs. their account average
- Topic / angle

After 100 posts collected → patterns emerge. Codify into `my-hook-banks/<niche>.md`.

## Legal essentials

### Copyright basics for theme pages

- **Fair use is narrower than people think.** "Transformative use" means meaningful commentary, criticism, or parody — NOT just adding text overlay to someone's clip.
- **Music**: 99% of popular songs are claimed by labels. Use Epidemic Sound / Artlist / YouTube Audio Library only for monetized content.
- **Stock footage**: license terms vary — read whether "broadcast" / "commercial" use is included. Most subscription stock includes social media commercial use.
- **AI-generated**: currently no copyright on pure AI output (US Copyright Office, 2023+). Means no one owns it — but also means YOU can't copyright it.

### DMCA takedown realities

- A takedown removes the post but doesn't strike unless the rights-holder escalates
- 3 strikes = account terminated on most platforms
- Counter-notice: only file if you genuinely have rights (perjury risk otherwise)
- Build pages on **multiple platforms** so a takedown wave on one doesn't kill your business

### Attribution best practices

When re-editing with permission:
- Tag creator's @handle in caption (first line, not buried)
- Add @handle as on-screen text for entire duration
- Include attribution in alt-text / description on Pinterest + YT
- Pin a comment crediting the creator
- Send creator the post link after going live (relationship builder → easier to ask for next clip)

## Sourcing pipeline blueprint (for one page)

```
Monday: 60-min sourcing block
  ├─ Pexels/Storyblocks: download 10 raw B-roll clips for the week
  ├─ Competitor swipe-file: review 20 top posts from past 7 days
  ├─ Hook bank: extract 5 new hook patterns from competitors
  └─ Permission DMs: send 3 to creators whose clips you want

Tuesday-Thursday: 30-min daily editing block
  ├─ Re-edit Tier 4 clips (permissioned): 1 per day
  ├─ Cut 9:16 + 1:1 + 1000×1500 from same idea
  ├─ Schedule via Metricool to all 7 platforms
  └─ Update content tracker

Friday: 60-min analytics + planning
  ├─ Pull weekly stats per platform
  ├─ Identify top 3 + bottom 3 posts
  ├─ Update hook bank with this week's wins
  └─ Plan next week's content calendar
```

Total time per page per week: **~6 hours**. Less if you outsource editing ($200-500/mo via Fiverr / OnlineJobs.ph).
