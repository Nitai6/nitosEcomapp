# Theme-Page Formulas — Hooks, Structures, CTAs

Proven, scraped, and tested formulas for theme pages across all 7 platforms. Copy these as starting templates, then layer your niche specifics.

## Section 1 — Hook formulas (the first 0.5–3 seconds)

### Universal hook patterns (work on any short-form platform)

**Curiosity-gap hooks**
1. "I just found out that [specific surprising fact]..."
2. "Nobody's talking about [thing], but [implication]..."
3. "The real reason [common outcome] happens isn't what you think..."
4. "Wait until you see what happens at [time-stamp / point]..."
5. "This is why [N]% of [group] [outcome]..."

**Loss-aversion hooks**
6. "Stop doing [common thing]. Do [alternative] instead."
7. "Things I wish I knew before [outcome]..."
8. "[Common belief] is actually destroying your [goal]..."
9. "If you [common action], stop. Here's why."
10. "I wasted [time/money] on [thing] before figuring this out."

**Authority / data hooks**
11. "I analyzed [N] [thing]. Here's what I found."
12. "After [N] years of [activity], the #1 thing I'd tell my younger self is..."
13. "[N]% of [group] do this wrong..."
14. "The [profession] told me to [thing]. I tried it for [time]. Here's what happened."

**Contrarian / pattern-interrupt hooks**
15. "Unpopular opinion: [bold statement]"
16. "[Mainstream advice] is wrong. Here's why."
17. "Everyone's talking about [X]. The real story is [Y]."
18. "I stopped [common practice] and [unexpected positive result]."

**Listicle hooks**
19. "[N] [things] that [outcome]:"
20. "[N] [tools/tips/tricks] in under [time]:"
21. "[Number] signs you're [state]:"
22. "[N] [thing] every [audience] should own:"

**Story / POV hooks**
23. "POV: you just [scenario]..."
24. "When [trigger] happens..."
25. "Imagine [scenario]. That's exactly what [comparison]."
26. "Last [time period], [unexpected event] happened..."

**Question hooks (use sparingly — direct Q can underperform statement)**
27. "Did you know [surprising fact]?"
28. "What if I told you [counterintuitive claim]?"
29. "How do [group] [outcome]? It's not what you think."

**Visual-only hooks (text-on-screen, no narration first 0.5s)**
30. Solid color frame + bold text: "This is illegal in [N] countries"
31. Static product shot + "Don't buy this until you read this"
32. Screen recording mid-action: "Watch what happens next"
33. Number countdown overlay: "5 things I'd do differently"

### Platform-specific hook adjustments

**TikTok**: visual-first matters most. The first FRAME (not first second) decides. Use static text overlay on a color background, OR drop into mid-action.

**Instagram Reels**: similar to TT but slightly more aesthetic-driven. Bold typography on first frame outperforms motion.

**YouTube Shorts**: hook can be slightly slower (1-2s) because feed pacing is gentler. Still need first 3s.

**YouTube long-form**: thumbnail + title carries the hook. First 30s of video must pay off the thumbnail or viewers bail.

**Twitter/X**: text-only hook = first line. Bold claim, single sentence, no setup.

**Bluesky**: earnest voice. "I've been thinking about..." outperforms "Hot take:".

**Facebook**: longer hooks tolerated (older audience reads more). Story openings work.

**Pinterest**: hook = pin's text overlay + title. Must be benefit-loaded ("How to...", "[N] ways to...", "DIY [thing] for under $X").

## Section 2 — Body structures

### Short-form video (TT/IG/Shorts) — 7-30s

**Structure A: Hook → Reveal → Payoff** (best for curiosity gap)
```
0-1s   Hook (visual + text)
1-3s   Setup (1 sentence context)
3-15s  Reveal (the thing the hook teased)
15-25s Payoff / lesson
25-30s CTA
```

**Structure B: Listicle** (best for "N things")
```
0-2s   Hook ("5 things every [audience] should own")
2-25s  Each item: 3-5s, on-screen number, quick narration + visual
25-30s "Save this for later" CTA
```

**Structure C: Story** (best for POV + transformation)
```
0-2s   Hook (POV scenario)
2-8s   Setup (the world before)
8-20s  Conflict / turning point
20-28s Resolution
28-30s Lesson + CTA
```

**Structure D: Tutorial** (best for "how to")
```
0-2s   Hook ("How to [outcome] in [time]")
2-25s  Step-by-step (3-5 steps, on-screen step number)
25-30s Final result + "Save this" CTA
```

**Structure E: Compilation / List with payoff at end** (best for retention)
```
0-2s   Hook ("These [things] will blow your mind. #5 is unreal.")
2-25s  Items 1-4 (3-5s each)
25-30s Item 5 (the promised "wait for it" payoff)
```

### YouTube long-form (8-15 min)

**The faceless documentary structure**:
```
0-30s    Cold open hook (1-2 of the most compelling visuals + claim)
30-90s   Setup ("In this video..." promise + why-watch)
90s-3min Section 1 (history / context)
3-7min   Section 2 (the meat — main content)
7-11min  Section 3 (case study / examples / counterpoints)
11-13min Section 4 (synthesis / takeaway)
13-15min Outro + CTA (subscribe + next video link)

Key: drop a "rehook" every 60-90s — visual change, sound effect, new claim
```

### Carousel (IG / LinkedIn) — 8-10 slides

```
Slide 1: Hook + visual (the swipe-trigger)
Slide 2: Promise / context
Slides 3-8: The substance (one idea per slide)
Slide 9: Recap / save-bait
Slide 10: CTA + soft pitch
```

### Pinterest pin

```
Image: vertical 1000×1500, bright, faces if relevant
Text overlay: benefit-loaded headline (5-9 words)
Title: SEO keywords first, benefit second (e.g. "Easy Air Fryer Recipes for Beginners | 30-Min Dinners")
Description: 100-300 chars with 5-10 niche keywords, soft CTA
Link: lands on a post/page with display ads / affiliate / lead magnet
```

### Twitter/X thread

```
T1 (hook): Result + promise. "I [achieved X] in [time]. Here's the playbook:"
T2: The big lesson / framework
T3-T(N-2): One actionable insight per tweet, numbered
T(N-1): Recap / synthesis
T(N): CTA — "If this helped, RT the first tweet + follow @[you] for more on [niche]. Free [resource] in bio."
```

### Bluesky post

Less formula-driven, more conversational. Best patterns:
- Single observation (300 char) ending in something mildly thought-provoking
- "Threading" via reply chains rather than dedicated thread tools
- Image + 1-2 sentence caption beats long text

## Section 3 — CTA formulas

### CTAs by goal

**Goal: comments (algorithm signal)**
- "What would you add?"
- "Agree or disagree?"
- "Drop a [emoji] if you've been there."
- "Tag someone who needs this."
- "What's your [niche] hot take?"

**Goal: saves (highest IG/Pinterest signal)**
- "Save this for later."
- "Save this — you'll need it when [scenario]."
- "Bookmark for [event/time]."

**Goal: shares (highest reach signal across platforms)**
- "Send this to a friend who [scenario]."
- "Share this with someone who [needs it]."
- "Tag a [audience] who needs to see this."

**Goal: profile click → bio link**
- "Free [resource] in bio."
- "Link in bio for the full breakdown."
- "I made a [thing] — link in bio."

**Goal: comment-trigger DM (the social-funnel mechanic)**
- "Comment '[word]' and I'll DM you the full [resource]."
- "Drop '[word]' below and I'll send the [thing]."
- "Type '[word]' if you want the [outcome]."
- (See `social-funnel` skill for the automation behind this.)

**Goal: subscribe (YouTube)**
- "Subscribe so you don't miss next week's [niche topic]."
- "More like this every [day] — subscribe."
- Show subscribe animation in last 5s
- Use end screens (last 20s) for subscribe + next-video CTA

**Goal: click (Pinterest)**
- The pin IS the CTA — title + image must promise what's at the link
- "Read the full [thing] →" on the destination page is implicit
- Test: include literal arrow on pin, vs. clean

### CTA placement rules

- One CTA per piece of content. Multiple CTAs = no action.
- Last 3-5 seconds for video, last slide for carousel, last tweet for thread, last sentence for post
- For Reels: also pin a comment with the CTA — drives 2-3× more action
- Never lead with CTA. Earn the right to ask by delivering value first.

## Section 4 — Caption formulas

### Short-form video captions

**Pattern A: Restate hook**
```
The [thing] no one talks about.

[1-2 sentences expanding the video's point]

[CTA]

#hashtag1 #hashtag2 #hashtag3
```

**Pattern B: Story extension**
```
This took me [time] to figure out:

[3-5 short-line breakdown of the lesson]

[CTA]

[hashtags]
```

**Pattern C: Comment-bait**
```
I'm probably going to get hate for this but —

[restated bold claim]

[Question to audience]

[hashtags]
```

### YouTube descriptions (long-form)

```
[1-2 line hook restated]

[3-5 sentences of value preview — what they'll get]

📌 TIMESTAMPS:
0:00 Intro
0:30 [Section 1]
3:00 [Section 2]
...

🔗 LINKS:
- [Affiliate / resource 1]
- [Affiliate / resource 2]
- [Newsletter / lead magnet]

[Generic SEO paragraph with 8-12 niche keywords woven naturally]

#niche #keyword1 #keyword2
```

### Pinterest descriptions

```
[Benefit-loaded sentence with primary keyword]. [Secondary benefit with keyword 2]. [Soft CTA — click through].

[5-10 niche keywords, comma separated, blended into a sentence]
```

### Twitter/X (the tweet IS the caption)

Single line of bold text. No fluff. No emojis (mostly). End with period.

## Section 5 — Hashtag formulas

| Platform | Count | Strategy |
|---|---|---|
| **TikTok** | 3-5 | Mix: 1-2 broad niche + 1-2 specific sub-niche + 1 trending |
| **Instagram** | 3-5 (Meta now downranks 30-tag stuffing) | 1-2 broad + 2-3 specific |
| **YouTube** | 3-5 in description, 1-2 in title | SEO-first, hashtag tertiary |
| **Twitter/X** | 0-1 | One per tweet max — more = spam signal |
| **Bluesky** | 1-3 | Topic tags only, no spam hashtags |
| **Facebook** | 1-3 | Low value, but 1-3 niche tags help in Group cross-discovery |
| **Pinterest** | 0-3 | Keywords > hashtags. Use keywords in title + description instead. |

**Building your hashtag bank**: 30 niche-relevant hashtags, organized by tier:
- Tier 1 (10): broad niche hashtags (1M+ posts) — for discovery
- Tier 2 (15): specific sub-niche (10k-1M posts) — your sweet spot
- Tier 3 (5): hyper-specific (under 10k posts) — easier to rank top-9

Rotate: pick 3-5 per post, never the same exact set twice in a row.

## Section 6 — Title + thumbnail formulas (YouTube)

### Title patterns
1. "I [action] for [time]. Here's what happened."
2. "The truth about [topic]"
3. "[Number] [things] that will [benefit]"
4. "Why [common belief] is wrong"
5. "How to [outcome] (without [common pain])"
6. "[Subject] vs [subject]: which is [criterion]?"
7. "I tested every [thing] — these are the best"
8. "What [authority] won't tell you about [topic]"

### Thumbnail design principles
- 1-2 elements only (face/object + 1 supporting element)
- High contrast (red/yellow accents on dark background works disproportionately)
- 3-5 word text overlay max, sans-serif, bold
- No tiny details — must read at thumbnail size
- A/B test 3 variants per video (YT native A/B test or TubeBuddy)

### CTR benchmarks (long-form)
- 4-6% CTR = baseline
- 6-10% CTR = good
- 10%+ CTR = great
- Below 4% = thumbnail/title problem, not content problem

## Section 7 — The 10 most-stolen formulas (study these on competitors)

These templates appear across virtually every successful theme page. Recognize them, use them honestly, twist them with your niche:

1. **The reveal**: cold-open with a surprising visual, build narrative back to it
2. **The transformation**: before/after with an arc between
3. **The countdown**: "5 things... #1 will surprise you" — pays off promise at end
4. **The myth-bust**: "Everyone says X. Actually Y."
5. **The behind-the-scenes**: "How [thing] is actually made/done"
6. **The reaction-to-reaction**: clip + your overlay reaction (especially TT/IG)
7. **The listicle-with-savings**: "Save this — [N] tips for [scenario]"
8. **The quiz / interactive**: "Can you guess [thing]?"
9. **The comparison**: "X vs Y: which wins?"
10. **The recap**: "[News/event] just happened. Here's what it means for [audience]."
