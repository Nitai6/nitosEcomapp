# Owner's Blog Rules (verbatim, consolidated)

## Cadence

- **18 blog posts per month** (mandatory)
- Schedule: Mon / Wed / Fri / Sun → 4 posts/week ≈ 18/month

## Topical focus

> Topical focus is mandatory. Recent Google algorithm updates have severely punished sites that publish high volumes of off-topic or generic content so it must be really in our niche.

## EEAT

- E — Experience
- E — Expertise
- A — Authoritativeness
- T — Trustworthiness

> Modern volume — highly researched expert-level articles will generate more traffic organically than 10 shallow ones.

## Length

- **2,000–6,550 words** per article
- Hard floor: 2,000
- Hard ceiling: 6,550

## Tone

- Mix of **informational and light-mooded**
- **Active tone** for a young audience
- Real value to the reader
- Mission: **help the customer**

## Title

- Eye-catching, fault-provoking, interrogating
- Unconventional, dramatic story-style
- **No "unlock" / "unlocking"**

## Headings

- **8–10 unique, interesting headings** minimum
- No H1 in body (H1 is the title only)

## Intro

- Optimistic + funny
- Contains the main keyword (once, naturally)

## Outro

- Informative + impressive ("samurai" close)

## Forbidden words/phrases

- "unlock" / "unlocking"
- "forbidden"
- "not highly informative"
- "non-values"

## Keywords

- ≥8 keywords per article (DataForSEO cluster)
- Low competition + medium intent
- Don't spam the main keyword

## Links

- **Internal:** product links (text, not images) — placed as helpful resources, not sales pitches
- **External:** nofollow links to Wikipedia + authoritative sources
- **YouTube:** embed the video itself, not just the link

## Formatting

- Bullet lists with relevant examples
- Comparison tables that deliver direct value
- White space + skimmable structure

## Source quality

- Reddit / Quora / YouTube / Google blog posts (extract paragraph → expand)
- For research-grade: **PubMed**, Apify scrape
- Owner's mission: "give highly informative, helpful content that is to help customers"
