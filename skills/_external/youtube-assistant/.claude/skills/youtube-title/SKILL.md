# YouTube Title Writer

Write YouTube titles that maximize click-through rate by generating curiosity, using proven formats, leveraging power words, and optimizing for scannability. A single title change can be the difference between a video dying and blowing up.

## Process

### Step 1: Gather Context

Ask the user:
1. **What is the video about?** Get the core topic, angle, or thesis.
2. **Who is the target audience?** (beginners, intermediate, experts, general)
3. **What niche is this for?** The niche determines which power words and formats perform best.
4. **What is the video format?** (tutorial, listicle, story, review, deep dive, case study, challenge, vlog)
5. **What is the key payoff or result?** What does the viewer get from watching? What transformation, insight, or outcome does the video deliver?
6. **Any specific details that make the video unique?** Numbers, timeframes, specific results, unusual methods, personal stakes.

### Step 2: Research (if topic/niche warrants it)

If the user wants help identifying what works in their niche, search for:
- Top-performing videos on the topic to identify outlier title patterns
- Common power words in the niche
- Title structures that consistently drive high views

### Step 3: Generate Titles

Using the frameworks below, generate **10-15 title options** organized by approach:
- 3-4 titles using **proven format modeling**
- 3-4 titles using **curiosity gap techniques**
- 3-4 titles using **power word optimization**
- 2-3 **wildcard titles** that combine multiple techniques or take an unexpected angle

### Step 4: Evaluate and Rank

Run every title through the **Title Evaluation Checklist** below. Score each title and present the top 5 ranked options with brief notes on why each works.

### Step 5: Refine

Ask the user which direction they like. Iterate with variations — rephrase, shorten, swap power words, reorder — until they have a title they love.

## The Five Title Principles

### Principle 1: Generate Curiosity (Open a Loop)

If people aren't clicking on your video, it's because the title doesn't generate enough curiosity. Your title must open a loop — plant a question in the viewer's mind that their brain needs to resolve.

**How curiosity works:** Our brains hate unfinished thoughts. When a title makes you ask "why?" or "how?" or "what happened?", you're driven to click to close the loop.

**Example:** Instead of "I Tried Filipino Food", write "I Flew 7,292 Miles to Eat Here." The second title makes you ask: Why did you fly so far? What kind of food? Was it worth it?

#### Curiosity Techniques

| Technique | What It Does | Example |
|-----------|-------------|---------|
| **Specificity gap** | Use a specific detail that demands explanation | "I Flew 7,292 Miles to Eat Here" |
| **Unexpected contrast** | Pair two things that don't obviously go together | "A Monk Taught Me How to Make $10K/Month" |
| **Implied stakes** | Hint at a big consequence without revealing it | "The Mistake That Almost Ended My Channel" |
| **Withhold the answer** | Promise a revelation but don't give it away | "I Found Out Why My Videos Stopped Growing" |
| **Challenge a belief** | Contradict something the audience assumes is true | "You Don't Need 10K Subscribers to Make Money" |
| **Tease a transformation** | Show the before and after, but leave out the how | "I Went From 0 to 100K Subscribers Using One Strategy" |
| **Create FOMO** | Make viewers feel they're missing something | "The YouTube Strategy Nobody's Talking About" |
| **Use open-ended numbers** | Numbers that raise more questions than they answer | "I Made $47,000 From a 3-Minute Video" |

#### Rules for Curiosity

- **Don't give away the answer.** If the title tells you everything, there's no reason to click.
- **Don't be vague.** "This Changed Everything" is not curiosity — it's nothing. Be specific enough to intrigue, vague enough to click.
- **Don't mislead.** The video must deliver on what the title implies. Broken trust = unsubscribes.

### Principle 2: Model Proven Formats

You don't have to invent brand new ways to write titles. Even the most successful YouTubers model proven formats — taking inspiration from titles that have already driven millions of views.

#### How to Model Titles

1. **Find outliers.** Look for videos that far outperformed a channel's view average. These are videos with significantly higher views than surrounding uploads.
2. **Extract the structure.** Break the outlier title down to its underlying format, removing the specific topic.
3. **Apply to your video.** Plug your topic into the proven structure.

#### Proven Title Formats

| Format | Structure | Example |
|--------|-----------|---------|
| **Bold action + timeframe + result** | I [bold action] for [time] — This Is What Happened | "I Woke Up at 4AM Every Day for a Month — This Is What Happened" |
| **Number + unexpected subject** | [Number] [Things] That [Unexpected Outcome] | "5 Free Apps That Replaced My $1,000 Setup" |
| **How I + specific result** | How I [Achieved Result] in [Timeframe/Constraint] | "How I Got 1 Million Views With 500 Subscribers" |
| **Why + contrarian claim** | Why [Common Advice] Is Wrong | "Why You Should Stop Posting Every Day" |
| **I tried + experiment** | I Tried [Thing] for [Duration] | "I Tried Living on $1 a Day for a Week" |
| **The truth about** | The Truth About [Common Belief] | "The Truth About YouTube Shorts" |
| **Stop doing X** | Stop [Common Mistake] (Do This Instead) | "Stop Using Hashtags (Do This Instead)" |
| **X vs Y** | [Option A] vs [Option B] — Which Is Better? | "iPhone vs Android — I Used Both for a Year" |
| **This is why** | This Is Why [Surprising Observation] | "This Is Why Your Videos Get No Views" |
| **What happens when** | What Happens When You [Bold Action] | "What Happens When You Post 3 Videos a Day" |

#### Rules for Modeling

- **Model the structure, not the words.** Don't copy titles — extract the underlying format and make it your own.
- **Use formats proven in YOUR niche.** A format that works in fitness may not work in finance.
- **Test multiple formats.** Generate titles using 3-4 different proven formats and compare.

### Principle 3: Use Power Words

One word or phrase could be the difference between getting clicks and getting completely ignored. Power words are specific words that make your title more intriguing without changing its meaning.

**Example:** "How to Edit and Film YouTube Videos" vs. "How to Edit and Film **Cinematic** Videos **Like a Pro**." Same video — dramatically different appeal.

#### Power Word Categories

| Category | What It Does | Examples |
|----------|-------------|---------|
| **Emotional** | Triggers a feeling that compels action | Shocking, Insane, Heartbreaking, Unbelievable, Mind-Blowing, Hilarious |
| **Authority** | Signals expertise and high-quality content | Pro, Expert, Masterclass, Ultimate, Advanced, Complete Guide |
| **Urgency** | Creates time pressure or fear of missing out | Now, Before It's Too Late, Stop, Don't, Must-Know, Warning |
| **Exclusivity** | Makes the viewer feel they're getting insider access | Secret, Hidden, Nobody Talks About, Underground, Little-Known |

#### Niche-Specific Power Words

Every niche has its own power words that drive clicks. These are words that consistently appear in top-performing titles within the niche.

| Niche | High-Performing Words |
|-------|----------------------|
| **Finance** | Broke, Millionaire, Passive Income, Side Hustle, Free, Wealth |
| **Fitness** | Shredded, Transformation, Fat Burning, Insane, Beast, Lean |
| **Tech** | Killer, Insane, Budget, Best, Ultimate, vs, Setup |
| **Gaming** | Broken, OP, Insane, Secret, Best, Clutch, World Record |
| **Cooking** | Easy, Perfect, Secret, Restaurant-Quality, Under [time], One-Pot |
| **Business** | Passive, Automated, $[amount], Blueprint, Strategy, Framework |
| **Education** | Easy, Simple, Explained, Complete, Beginner, Why, How |

**Finding your niche's power words:** Scroll through the top-performing videos in your niche and look for patterns. The same words appear over and over in titles that get high views.

#### Rules for Power Words

- **Replace bland words, don't add extra ones.** Swap "good" for "incredible," don't add "incredible" on top of an already long title.
- **Use 1-2 power words max per title.** More than that feels clickbaity.
- **Match the word to the content.** Don't use "insane" if the video is a calm tutorial.

### Principle 4: Optimize Title Length and Word Order

Your title isn't viewed in a vacuum — it's surrounded by dozens of other videos competing for attention. If your title takes too long to process, the viewer's eyes will be drawn to a title with faster intake speed.

#### Length Guidelines

- **Aim for under 55 characters.** This ensures the full title displays on all devices.
- **Longer titles CAN work** but only if structured so the core message is clear from the first half alone.
- **On some devices, YouTube cuts off titles** with "..." — never let the most curiosity-evoking part get cut.

#### Word Order Rules

People don't read word by word — they skim. The first words of your title do the heaviest lifting.

- **Frontload the hook.** Put the most important, curiosity-driving words at the beginning.
- **Core message first, context second.** The viewer should understand what the video is about from the first 5-6 words alone.

**Example:**
- Bad: "My Experience Trying to Learn How to Do a Backflip in 30 Days" (the interesting part — "backflip in 30 days" — is buried)
- Good: "I Learned to Backflip in 30 Days" (hook is immediate)

**Example:**
- Bad: "Everything I've Learned About Building a Successful YouTube Channel After 5 Years" (YouTube may cut this to "Everything I've Learned About Building a Succ...")
- Good: "5 Years on YouTube — Here's What Actually Works" (core message survives any truncation)

#### Quick Tests

- **The skim test:** Read only the first 5 words. Do you know what the video is about?
- **The truncation test:** Cut the title in half. Does the first half still make sense and generate curiosity?
- **The comparison test:** Place your title next to 5 competitor titles. Does it stand out?

### Principle 5: Be Willing to Change a Title That's Not Working

Sometimes despite your best efforts, a title doesn't perform. That's okay — but you need to be willing to change it. Big creators change titles constantly.

#### When to Change a Title

Monitor **click-through rate (CTR)** and **impressions** after upload:

| Scenario | Action |
|----------|--------|
| CTR AND impressions both below average in the first 24 hours | Something about your title + thumbnail isn't resonating. Change one. Titles are easier to change than thumbnails. |
| CTR and impressions are average | Hold off on changes. Wait until after your video's typical traction window before making adjustments. |
| CTR is high but impressions are low | Title is compelling but YouTube isn't pushing the video. May need a broader or more searchable title. |
| Impressions are high but CTR is low | YouTube is showing the video but people aren't clicking. Title needs more curiosity or a stronger hook. |

#### How Long to Wait

- Find your channel's typical traction pattern. Some channels pick up at day 2, others at day 8-10.
- Go into YouTube Studio, check the "Typical in this period" view range, and look for where the slope changes.
- Don't make major changes before that window. After it passes, test new titles.
- It can take up to a week or more to see results from a title change — be patient.

## Title Evaluation Checklist

Score every title candidate on these criteria before presenting to the user.

### Curiosity Check
- [ ] Does the title open a loop? Does it make you ask a question?
- [ ] Is the answer withheld? (If the title tells you everything, there's no reason to click)
- [ ] Would someone who sees this in their feed feel compelled to find out more?

### Clarity Check
- [ ] Can you understand what the video is about from the first 5-6 words?
- [ ] Does the title match what the video actually delivers?
- [ ] Would the target audience immediately recognize this is for them?

### Length & Format Check
- [ ] Is it under 55 characters? (If longer, does the first half survive truncation?)
- [ ] Are the most important words frontloaded?
- [ ] Does it pass the skim test?

### Power & Impact Check
- [ ] Does the title use at least one power word or curiosity-driving phrase?
- [ ] Are the word choices specific and vivid (not generic)?
- [ ] Does it stand out compared to competitor titles on the same topic?

### Honesty Check
- [ ] Does the video deliver on what the title promises?
- [ ] Would a viewer feel satisfied — not misled — after watching?
- [ ] Is there a real payoff behind the curiosity gap?

## Title Scoring System

Rate each title on a 1-5 scale across five dimensions:

| Dimension | 1 (Weak) | 3 (Average) | 5 (Strong) |
|-----------|----------|-------------|------------|
| **Curiosity** | No question raised, title tells everything | Some intrigue but not compelling | Impossible to NOT want to click |
| **Clarity** | Confusing or vague, unclear what video is about | Understandable but generic | Instantly clear and specific |
| **Scannability** | Long, buried hook, poor word order | Decent but could be tighter | Short, punchy, frontloaded hook |
| **Power** | Bland word choices, no emotional pull | Some appeal but forgettable | Vivid, specific, niche-appropriate power words |
| **Differentiation** | Looks like every other video on this topic | Somewhat unique angle | Clearly stands out from competitors |

**Total score out of 25.** Titles scoring 20+ are strong candidates. Below 15, rework them.

## Common Title Mistakes (Quick Reference)

Use this as a sanity check against every title you generate:

| Mistake | What It Looks Like | Fix |
|---------|-------------------|-----|
| **No reason to click** | "I Tried Filipino Food" | Add specificity and stakes: "I Flew 7,292 Miles to Eat Here" |
| **Not using proven formats** | Inventing from scratch every time | Find outliers, extract the structure, apply to your topic |
| **Boring words** | "How to Edit YouTube Videos" | Swap in power words: "How to Edit Cinematic Videos Like a Pro" |
| **Too wordy** | "My Complete Experience Learning Everything About Starting a YouTube Channel" | Cut to core: "What I Learned Starting YouTube From Zero" |
| **Burying the hook** | "After 6 Months of Hard Work I Finally Hit 100K Subscribers" | Frontload: "0 to 100K Subscribers in 6 Months" |
| **Giving away the answer** | "The Best Camera for YouTube Is the Sony A7IV" | Withhold: "I Tested Every YouTube Camera — One Blew Me Away" |
| **Never changing it** | Keeping a bad title because you're attached to it | Monitor CTR, test alternatives, iterate |

## Guidelines

- Always generate multiple options — never present just one title.
- Prioritize curiosity above all else. A clear title that generates zero curiosity will not get clicks.
- Tailor power words and formats to the specific niche. What works in fitness doesn't always work in finance.
- Shorter is almost always better. If you can say it in fewer words, do it.
- The title and thumbnail work as a pair. Ask the user about their thumbnail concept so the title complements (not repeats) the visual.
- When in doubt, read the title out loud. If it doesn't sound like something you'd tell a friend, rewrite it.
